Nepal's Unsung Heroes

Date Fri 29 May 2015 Tags aljazeera

Editor's note: This film will be available online from May 29, 2015.

Since a 7.8 magnitude earthquake devastated Nepal, the young teacher has been leading a relief campaign to provide help.

His plea on social media for urgent aid has him answering calls around the clock, making arduous journeys up remote mountain roads to deliver supplies to his students' villages in the worst-hit district of Sindhupalchok.

Political instability is hampering government and international aid.

101 East follows one man's mission to restore hope to survivors of Nepal's worst earthquake in more than 80 years.

Survivor story: The man who lost everything

By Bibek Bhandari

On a humid afternoon in May, 28-year-old Uddhav Paudel sits, like a defeated warrior, on a pile of rubble that he once called home.

His maternal grandmother along with his brother-in-law and grandmother-in-law were also killed.

"In a matter of seconds my entire world turned around."

The drive to his small village in the district of Sindhupalchowk is striking - a stark paradox between nature's beauty versus the power of its devastation.

The view of the snow-capped Himalayas, rolling hills and gushing rivers is eclipsed by flattened villages and collapsed homes.

They were constructed using cement and bricks as opposed to stone and mud.

He finds a withered school backpack crushed beneath a pile of rocks, and a notebook scribbled with lines that read, "My country['s] name is Nepal."

At 11:56am, the earth suddenly began shaking.

So he cuddled his younger son and started praying while his wife Meena and elder son stood by.

His younger son buried outside the porch.

One month later, Paudel is struggling to cope.

"It's a different world altogether.

The World Health Organization has reported a rise in mental health problems following the disaster.

In remote areas people are less worried about mental health.

But many fear bureaucracy and red tape will delay these benefits.

The future, he said, looks bleak.

"But everything has changed.
